Hollywood, 1953. The screens flash with the dazzling sparkle of diamonds, and the air is filled with the aroma of champagne and a slight spirit of adventure.

“Gentlemen Prefer Blondes” is not just a film, it is a whirlwind of sparkling humor, mesmerizing beauty and unbridled fun, shrouded in an atmosphere of glamor and luxury.

Some like it hot

"Only girls in Jazz" is not just a movie, but a whirlwind of sparkling humor, incendiary music and dazzling beauty. The story of two musicians forced to disguise themselves as women in order to continue their careers unfolds like a fairy tale full of unexpected twists and irresistible charm. Every note of jazz, every dance step, every look is imbued with the lightness and unique charm of the old Hollywood era.

This is a movie that makes the heart beat to the beat of a cheerful melody, charges with positivity and leaves behind a taste of sweet retro chic and unforgettable impressions.

The film is a vivid festival of life, love and music, where laughter is intertwined with romance, and the illusion turns into an exciting adventure.

How to Marry a Millionaire

"How to Marry a Millionaire" is not just a movie, but an exquisite cocktail of gloss, wit and the elusive charm of the golden era of Hollywood. Three charming girls dreaming of a luxurious life are building a bold and sophisticated plan to capture the hearts (and fortunes) of rich bachelors.

The film immerses the viewer in the atmosphere of luxurious apartments, brilliant parties and mesmerizing fashion, painting a cheerful picture of female friendship, cunning intrigues and unpredictable turns of fate. This is a light, playful comedy, imbued with optimism and faith in a miracle, where every scene is filled with style, humor and the unchanging charm of old Hollywood.

The film leaves behind a pleasant feeling of lightness, joy and undying faith in a happy ending.
